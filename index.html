01

What is VALMO-X?

VALMO-X is a retrofit-compatible smart condition monitoring system for industrial pneumatic globe control valves operating under ANSI 150 RF specifications. The name stands for VALve MOnitoring eXtended.

The core problem it solves: traditional industrial valve maintenance is either purely reactive (fix after failure) or schedule-based (replace on a calendar, regardless of actual wear). Both approaches are expensive — reactive causes unplanned downtime, schedule-based wastes good components.

VALMO-X enables predictive maintenance. By continuously monitoring pressure, temperature, vibration and valve position, applying a physics-based coupling model, and running a real-time fault classification engine, it detects degradation weeks or months before failure — and tells you exactly how many hours of useful life remain.

The entire system runs as a single-file HTML dashboard — no server needed — receiving live sensor data via Node-RED WebSocket from existing plant infrastructure.

02

Target Hardware — GCV-10 Valve

The dashboard monitors a GCV-10 ANSI 150 RF Pneumatic Globe Control Valve with a WCB (Cast Carbon Steel) body — the most common configuration in oil & gas, petrochemical, and process industries worldwide.

⚙️

ANSI 150 RF (Raised Face)

ANSI/ASME B16.5 Class 150 pressure–temperature rating. Raised face flanges ensure a tight metallic seal. WCB Carbon Steel rated to 285 psi at 100°F, pressure derated to 0 psi at 800°F. Maximum working temperature for WCB body: 500°C (932°F).

🔧

Pneumatic Actuator (Spring-Return)

Air-to-open or air-to-close spring-return diaphragm actuator. Supply pressure 20–100 psi air. Positioner (electro-pneumatic) converts 4–20 mA control signal to air pressure for precise stem positioning. Failure of the actuator or pneumatic supply causes position lag — a key fault signature monitored by VALMO-X.

🔩

Globe Valve Trim

Plug-and-seat trim design. Flow coefficient Cv scales with plug position according to ISA-75.01 equal-percentage or linear characteristic. Trim wear leads directly to seat leakage — the second fault mode monitored continuously by VALMO-X.

VALMO-X fuses data from exactly four sensor parameters. These were chosen because together they span all physically observable fault signatures for this valve class, while remaining practical and cost-effective for a retrofit installation.

05

Bi-Directional Physics Coupling Engine

In a real valve installation, no sensor operates in isolation. A pressure spike changes the temperature; temperature raises pressure; vibration from turbulence shifts the plug position. VALMO-X models 10 physical cross-couplings between all 4 sensor parameters, applied in sequence on every data cycle. This eliminates false alarms from coupled effects and provides early composite warnings that no single sensor would detect alone.

Example cascade: Pressure rises to 200 psi → compression heating adds +21.6°C to temperature → elevated temperature raises pressure by +0.8 psi → high pressure drives turbulence, adding +0.21g vibration → vibration chatter drifts position by +0.35% → position throttling adds +1.2°C friction heat. All five events are logged individually with their coupling formula in the Event Log.

06

Fault Diagnosis — 4 Failure Modes

The Fault Diagnosis Engine runs on every physics update cycle and scores each of the four documented failure modes. Scores range 0–100; thresholds at 30 (early warning) and 60 (confirmed fault) trigger log events and badge colour changes.

FAULT MODE 01
⚙ Stiction Detection
Stick-slip friction in the valve stem packing or positioner causes the plug to stick, then jump erratically when force builds enough to overcome friction. Invisible on a single snapshot — only detectable through position oscillation patterns over time.
Primary signature: Position hunting — repeated direction reversals without setpoint change. Scored by counting direction-change cycles.
Secondary signature: Erratic vibration spikes (Vib > 0.8g) coinciding with position jumps — mechanical energy release from stick-to-slip transition.
Score formula: (hunting cycles × 8) + vibration penalty + position deviation penalty. Max 100.
Action threshold: Score > 60 → schedule positioner repacking or trim cleaning.
FAULT MODE 02
💧 Seat Leakage
Wear or erosion of the valve seat and plug trim allows fluid to flow even when the valve is commanded fully closed. Classified by ANSI/FCI 70-2 leakage classes I through VI. VALMO-X detects and classifies this in real time.
Primary signature: Non-zero flow Cv (residual flow coefficient) when position deviation is < 2% (near-shutoff condition).
Leakage classes: SEALED (score <20), CLASS III (20–50), CLASS II (50–75), CLASS I LEAK (≥75).
Shutoff integrity: Calculated from residual Cv and upstream pressure. Decreases as leakage worsens.
Hazard: Undetected seat leakage causes production loss, cross-contamination, or safety-critical fluid escape.
FAULT MODE 03
🌬 Actuator Air Leak
Diaphragm rupture, tubing fitting failure, or positioner seal wear causes pneumatic supply pressure to decay. The actuator becomes sluggish, unable to move the stem at normal speed — increasing risk of uncontrolled shutdown.
Primary signature: Position lag — the stem position deviates from setpoint and responds slowly, especially during rapid setpoint changes.
Pressure decay metric: Supply pressure drop rate (psi/min) computed from score. Normal: <1.5 psi/min; Fault: >4.8 psi/min.
Stroke response states: NORMAL → SLUGGISH (score 30–60) → LAGGING (score >60).
Compounding condition: High vibration with near-zero position movement = stiff/locked actuator, highest risk state.
FAULT MODE 04
⚠ Mechanical Degradation
Cumulative damage from thermal cycling, pressure pulsation, and vibration causes invisible wear accumulation in packing, trim, bearings, and body. This is the basis for Remaining Useful Life estimation — the most industrially valuable output of VALMO-X.
Wear scoring: Incremental score accumulates every cycle based on operating conditions above warning thresholds: Vib (+0.06/cycle), T (+0.08/cycle), P (+0.04/cycle), Pos (+0.05/cycle).
Severity levels: HEALTHY (0–25%), WEAR ONSET (25–50%), DEGRADED (50–75%), CRITICAL WEAR (75–100%).
RUL output: Exponential degradation model feeds directly into Remaining Useful Life estimate (see Section 07).
Early action window: Alert triggers at score > 50% — provides 25–50% remaining life for planned shutdown scheduling.
07

Remaining Useful Life (RUL) Estimation

VALMO-X estimates RUL using an exponential degradation model — the industry-standard approach for mechanical wear processes where failure rate increases with accumulated damage. The model assumes a maximum design life of 8,760 hours (1 year) for continuous service, consistent with typical industrial valve inspection intervals.

The Formula

RUL = MaxLife × e−k × D

Where:
MaxLife = 8760 h (1 year)
k = 0.046 (decay constant)
D = degradScore (0 – 100)

Worked Examples

Degrad Score Severity RUL Output
5% Healthy 6,923 h ≈ 9.6 mo
25% Wear Onset 2,866 h ≈ 4.0 mo
50% Degraded 939 h ≈ 39 d
75% Critical Wear 308 h ≈ 13 d
100% End of Life 101 h ≈ 4 d

The RUL is displayed in three locations on the dashboard: the header stat bar, the system health panel (centre column), and the Mechanical Degradation fault card. The display format automatically scales: months (mo) when > 720h, days (d) when > 24h, and hours (h) for urgent end-of-life conditions.

System Architecture — 4 Layers

VALMO-X follows a four-layer IIoT architecture, from physical sensors through edge processing to the analytics engine and final visualisation. The design is retrofit-first: it plugs into existing plant infrastructure without replacing any installed hardware.

L1
Sensor Field Layer — Physical Measurements
Pressure transmitter, temperature transmitter (RTD/TC), MEMS accelerometer on valve body, and smart positioner feedback. All wired to field junction box. Signals are 4–20 mA loops with optional HART digital overlay for diagnostic data.
4–20 mA / HART Pt100 RTD Type K Thermocouple MEMS Accelerometer LVDT Position
L2
Edge / Gateway Layer — Node-RED
Node-RED flow running on a Raspberry Pi or industrial gateway (Siemens IOT2040, etc.) reads the 4–20 mA signals via I/O modules, applies engineering unit scaling, runs threshold checks, and publishes JSON payloads over WebSocket on port 1880. Four sensor flows plus four fault simulation flows for testing.
Node-RED 3.x WebSocket /ws/valve JSON payload Raspberry Pi 4 Siemens IOT2040
L3
Analytics Layer — Physics + Fault Engine (Browser)
The VALMO-X dashboard JavaScript receives WebSocket frames and runs: (1) 10-coupling bi-directional physics engine, (2) four fault diagnosis algorithms with real-time scoring, (3) exponential RUL estimator, (4) health index calculation, (5) sparkline history buffers, (6) CSV export logger. All computation runs client-side — no cloud dependency.
Physics Coupling Engine Fault Scoring RUL Model Vanilla JavaScript Zero backend
L4
Visualisation Layer — VALMO-X Dashboard
Single-file HTML dashboard rendering: 4 sensor cards with sparkline charts, live 3D Three.js valve model with water flow animation, system health arc gauge, 4 fault diagnosis cards with progress bars, RUL widget, event log, maintenance mode overlay, and CSV export. Responsive, no framework, no build step.
Three.js r128 3D Model HTML5 Canvas Charts 4 Sensor Cards 4 Fault Cards CSV Export

Node-RED JSON payload format: {"pressure":120, "temperature":80, "vibration":0.1, "position":1}

09

Dashboard Features

📊

4 Sensor Cards

Each card shows: live big-number value, unit with sub-type, derived SI conversions, colour-coded progress bar, warn/crit threshold tags, 30-point sparkline history, fault coupling note. Cards flash red with animation on CRITICAL.

🎮

Live 3D Valve Model

Three.js 3D globe valve with animated water flow particles, stem movement based on position reading, LED status indicator, overlay tags for all 4 sensor live values, and burst/fault animation on critical events.

💚

System Health Index

SVG arc gauge 0–100%. Calculated as the average of all four sensor status scores (100 = normal, 60 = warning, 10 = critical). Turns amber at <70%, red at <40%. Also shown as large numeric + state label.

⏳

RUL Widget

Remaining Useful Life display with hours/days/months formatting, colour-coded progress bar, and degradation rate label. Appears in both the health row and the header stat bar for at-a-glance visibility.

🔍

Fault Diagnosis Panel

Four fault cards (Stiction, Seat Leakage, Air Leak, Mech Degradation) each showing: status badge, two metric rows, RUL progress bar, and coupling note. Cards border-highlight by severity with pulsing animation on confirmed fault.

📋

Event Log

Timestamped scrollable log with colour-coded entries: INFO (blue), WARNING (amber), CRITICAL (red), OK (green), DERIVED (grey for physics coupling events). Every coupling, fault detection, and threshold breach is logged automatically.

🔌

WebSocket Live Connection

Connect to any Node-RED WebSocket endpoint. Dot indicator shows CONNECTED (green, blinking) / DISCONNECTED (red) / CONNECTING (amber). URL editable in dashboard. Auto-reconnect on disconnect.

🔧

Maintenance Mode

Toggle button pauses monitoring and shows a high-visibility striped banner. Logs maintenance start/end. All sensor updates continue recording but alerts are suppressed during scheduled maintenance windows.

💾

CSV Export

One-click export of all recorded sensor data with timestamps, raw values, physics-corrected values, fault scores, and RUL estimates. Accumulates up to 10,000+ rows during a monitoring session.
